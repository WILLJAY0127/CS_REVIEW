**"Nothing" does NOT always mean zero or empty.**

It depends on the context!

---

**Look at these examples:**

| Sentence | Meaning |
|---|---|
| "There is nothing in the box." | The box is empty ❌ |
| "Nothing too difficult." | Not very difficult 😊 |
| "Nothing special." | Not very special |
| "Nothing much." | Not a lot |

---

**"Nothing too difficult" is a short form of:**

> "It is **nothing** that is **too difficult**."
> = "It is **not** too difficult."

💡 Think of it like this in code:
```
nothing too difficult = not (too difficult) = easy enough
```

---

**More natural examples like this:**

- *"How are you?"* → **"Nothing bad!"** = I'm fine
- *"Hard project?"* → **"Nothing too serious."** = Not too serious
- *"Big problem?"* → **"Nothing I can't handle."** = I can handle it

---

**In short:**
> "Nothing + adjective" in conversation usually means **"not very..."**

It is a very natural, casual English pattern. Native speakers use it a lot! 😄
